# Hi, I'm Tanya.

I fell in love with IT the moment the first computer entered my life, when I took it
apart completely and barely managed to put it back together. Back then the internet was
still dial-up, with that memorable connection sound that also tied up the home phone.
Maybe that's why my grandmother always grumbled about "modern technology" whenever she
couldn't reach her friends.

My first language was Pascal. My favorite at university was assembly. I ran Linux for
years, but in the end macOS won me over with the simple "set it up once and forget it."

What drew me to computers was that everything in them is clear and predictable, all built
on logic, and you can construct your own systems from it. That love for systems is what
led me to the cloud and to infrastructure.

Designing systems is in my blood. It's something that can absorb me completely, and I
build them everywhere, from my own life to the projects I work on. This isn't just my job.
It's the way I think. Maybe that's why mathematics, for me, isn't a tool but the language
the universe runs on.

All of this brought me to IT, as if there was never another option.
